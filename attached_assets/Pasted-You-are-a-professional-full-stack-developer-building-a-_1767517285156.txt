You are a professional full-stack developer building a working AI-native web application, not a static or text-only demo.

App Name
SenseBite
Tagline: Making sense of every bite

Core Objective
Build a functional web app that demonstrates an AI-native consumer health experience where AI is the primary interface, not an add-on.

The app helps users understand food ingredients at the moment of decision by reasoning and explanation, not by listing data.

This is not a database browser, nutrition tracker, or ingredient lookup tool.

Key Experience Rules (Must Follow)
AI must infer what matters without asking setup questions

No filters, profiles, checkboxes, or configuration screens

No ingredient scores, red/green flags, or health ratings

No dashboards or feature menus

Avoid treating AI like a chatbot pasted into a page

AI must do cognitive work on behalf of the user

User Flow (Required)
Entry
User can:

Paste an ingredient list as text

OR upload an image of a food label (OCR can be mocked or basic)

No onboarding, login, or setup screens.

AI Behavior (Most Important)
Once ingredients are provided, the AI must:

Respond immediately without asking questions

Identify only 2–3 ingredients that matter most

Explain each in simple human language:

Why it’s used

What trade-off exists

Where uncertainty remains

Avoid medical or definitive claims

Use a calm, non-judgmental tone

The AI should not list all ingredients.

Follow-Up Interaction
Allow users to ask natural follow-up questions such as:

“Why is this ingredient used?”

“Is this okay if I eat it often?”

“Are there simpler alternatives?”

The AI should adapt its explanation based on user follow-ups without restarting the flow.

Reasoning & Explainability Requirement
Include an explanation layer where the AI briefly clarifies:

Why it focused on certain ingredients

What assumptions it made

Where scientific certainty is limited

This must be written in plain language.

Uncertainty & Safety
The AI must:

Explicitly communicate uncertainty when appropriate

Avoid absolute statements

Include a small disclaimer:
“SenseBite provides clarity, not medical advice.”

Technical Expectations
Use a modern web stack (React / Next.js or equivalent)

Backend API for AI reasoning

LLM-based responses (no fine-tuning required)

Ingredient data can be simulated or partial

OCR accuracy is not important

Focus on experience quality over data completeness

Required Pages / Sections
Main Experience Page

Ingredient input

AI reasoning output

Follow-up interaction

Short “About” section

Explains philosophy in 2–3 sentences

Small disclaimer section

No user accounts.
No saved history.

Anti-Goals (Explicitly Do NOT Build)
Ingredient databases

Nutrition charts or tables

Health scoring systems

Research paper dumps

Filter-heavy interfaces

AI chat that feels generic or robotic

Success Criteria
The app should clearly show:

AI reasoning instead of data display

Reduced user confusion at decision time

A new interaction paradigm that could not exist without AI

If a judge sees the app without explanation, it should be obvious that:

“This experience is AI-native, not AI-added.”

Final Instruction
Build a working, interactive prototype that prioritizes reasoning clarity, user understanding, and intelligent behavior over visual complexity or dataset scale.
