# SenseBite  
**Making sense of every bite**

SenseBite is an **AI-native consumer health experience** that helps people understand food ingredients at the exact moment decisions are made. Instead of listing data or nutrition tables, SenseBite focuses on **reasoning, clarity, and context** — translating complex ingredient labels into calm, human explanations.

---

## 🧠 What SenseBite Does

Food labels are optimized for compliance, not understanding. SenseBite acts as a **co-pilot**, helping users make sense of what actually matters in a product without asking them to configure filters, profiles, or preferences.

SenseBite:
- Explains ingredients in **plain, human language**
- Highlights **trade-offs** instead of good/bad labels
- Communicates **uncertainty honestly**
- Reduces cognitive load at decision time

---

## ✨ Key Features

- **AI-native interaction**  
  The AI is the interface — not an add-on.

- **Ingredient reasoning, not data dumping**  
  Only what matters is explained, naturally.

- **Humanized explanations**  
  Calm, conversational responses instead of technical breakdowns.

- **Clickable product categories**  
  Explore common product types (cereals, snacks, drinks, etc.) by clicking an image — no search or typing required.

- **No scoring or judgments**  
  SenseBite does not label food as “good” or “bad” and does not provide medical advice.

---

## 🚫 What SenseBite Is Not

- ❌ Not a nutrition tracker  
- ❌ Not a database browser  
- ❌ Not an ingredient lookup tool  
- ❌ Not a health scoring app  
- ❌ Not an AI chatbot pasted onto a form  

The experience is designed around **understanding**, not information overload.

---

## 🧩 How It Works (Conceptually)

1. User pastes ingredients or selects a product category  
2. The system infers what matters without asking setup questions  
3. AI reasons internally and produces a **single human explanation**  
4. User gains clarity and confidence

The data source is secondary — the experience is the product.

---

## 🗂️ Data & Grounding

SenseBite may optionally use:
- Public food datasets (e.g. Open Food Facts)
- Small curated context sets for ingredient understanding

These sources are **never shown directly to the user**.  
The value lies in **reasoning and explanation**, not data completeness.

---

## ⚠️ Disclaimer

SenseBite provides **clarity, not medical advice**.  
All explanations are informational and intended to support understanding, not diagnosis or treatment.
